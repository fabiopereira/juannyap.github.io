Overview, Objectives and Deliverables

What is the GoFar problem?

Decide which target audience works better in instance to sell the product and settle in the market.

The technology OBD 2 (On Boarding Diagnosys) started to grow slowly in the market in 2013. Today, 2015, it is still not well known.

User, Product and Competitive Research

What is it that GoFar does?

On-board diagnostics (OBD) is an automotive term referring to a vehicle's ability of self-diagnostic and reporting. GoFar connects your mobile phone with your engine so it can give the vehicle owner or repair technician access to the status of the various vehicle subsystems. It can also give you instant feedback on the way you drive helping you save money.

Who is their current target audience?

They want to reach as many people as possible when they first launch, so they thought about the normal consumers. Although the idea seems great we found that it could be improved.

Who & how do people use their mobile inside the car?

A online survey with 53 responses gave us great insight into what was needed for an ideal mobile app.

...we had responses from all over the world.

Our responses came from Latin America, USA, Europe, Oceania and Asia, from engineers to babysitters.

Who competes with GoFar?

We had a heuristic look at competitors sites to see what was on offer.

There are not many good competitors in Sydney.

We researched the competitors online and found that most competitors are overseas.

How do people want to receive instant feedback?

33.9% prefered voice and 30.4% prefered sound.

Our client and user research meant we knew what pages were needed to create the ideal user flows. We iterated many times with the aim of trying to simplify the process where possible.

Scholastic

Brief

Scholastic has a problem increasing kids reading engagement outside class hours.

Client

Scholastic is a international publisher. This client was part of a project provided by General Assembly.

Duration

3 weeks

Team

  • Alina Van
  • Juanny Andrade
  • Malcom Stephens

Our Approach

User and competitor research, personas, sketching, site map, wireframing, prototyping, testing, iterating and presenting.

Tools

Axure, gizmo surveys, keynote, sharpies, sticky notes & paper.

User and competitor research

What does Scholastic want? ...the client wants to increase reading engagement & by consequence their revenue using technology.

How is the current website?

We analysed the Scholastic current website. We found several areas that could be improved....long & clunky! The existent Scholastic website needed too much changes.

The IA was poor, as well as the design. It is easy to get lost.

Online survey

After searching what works in terms of kids reading engagement, we went to the online survey targeting the teachers. We asked them various questions, including how was their relationship with technology and what they think about it.

Who competes with Scholastic?

We had a heuristic look at competitors sites to see what was on offer and how similar they were to Scholastic.

Target audience

Parents or teachers? The questions was simply answered when we realise how many kids each one of those could reach.

Personas

We created three personas that covered the majority of users we had found in the research stage.

User flows and scenarios

Scholastic existent website would be of no help for us so we decide to create something new. A desktop platform that would help teachers to help their students. We also created one scenario for each persona so it could give us a better understanding of the process.

We iterated many times with the aim of trying to simplify the process where possible.

Ideation and features prioritisation

There were tons of features we wanted to put in Magno. Each one of them with a good rational and usability but we did want to overwhelm our users. So we did a matrix with our features prioritisation.

MVP

The feature prioritisation led us to find what was our minimum viable product.

site map

Our research and mvp led us to a sitemap.

Our sitemap was created and we could now start designing the site pages.

sketching

How do we create a site which helps all the kind of teachers?

We cannot help every single user but we can create a solution that works for a broad range of them.

What about the information architecture?

Having done the user flows we could now map out the pages that were needed for the Magno website.

Sketches of site pages

With sharpies and paper we then sketched low fi site pages, re-iterated and tested. ...led to the start of Axure prototyping. Once we were happy with the sketches we then started wireframing in Axure.

testing, iterating and presenting

We tested the early prototype and iterated... The early prototype required a few changes after testing with users. ...this took us to the final Axure prototype. The clickable prototype was completed and ready to show to the clients.
